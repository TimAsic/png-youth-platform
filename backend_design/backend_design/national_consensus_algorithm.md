National Consensus Algorithm

Purpose

The National Consensus Algorithm determines how strongly a proposal is supported across Papua New Guinea.

The goal is to measure national agreement while accounting for:

- Population
- Provincial representation
- Participation rates
- Proposal quality

---

Core Principle

Consensus is not simply the number of supporters.

Consensus is a combination of:

- Support
- Geographic diversity
- Participation
- Proposal quality

---

National Support Score

NationalSupportScore (NSS)

NSS = TotalSupport / ActiveUsers

Purpose:

Measure overall support relative to platform participation.

---

Provincial Representation Score

PRS =

SupportingProvinces / TotalProvinces

Purpose:

Measure geographic reach.

A proposal supported in many provinces receives a higher score.

---

Participation Adjustment Factor

PAF =

ActiveUsers / YouthPopulation

Purpose:

Adjust for differences in participation levels.

---

Proposal Quality Score

PQS =

FeasibilityScore

+ ImpactScore

+ UrgencyScore

Purpose:

Reward useful proposals instead of popularity alone.

---

National Consensus Index

NCI =

(NSS × 0.35)

+ (PRS × 0.25)

+ (PAF × 0.15)

+ (PQS × 0.25)

Purpose:

Create a balanced measure of national consensus.

---

Interpretation

NCI > 80

Strong National Consensus

NCI 60–80

Moderate National Consensus

NCI 40–60

Emerging Consensus

NCI < 40

Limited Consensus
